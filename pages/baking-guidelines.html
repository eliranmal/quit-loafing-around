things to mind when getting to the actual baking:


bake time
bake times are always estimated.
as general rule of thumb (and unless stated otherwise), the bread is ready when the bottom crust is crisp and hard, and knocking on it produces a hollow sound.

if you have a spike thermometer, check that the loaf center had reached its target temperature; at least 92°C for white yeast breads, higher for sourdough breads or wetter doughs, and up to 108-112°C for more battery, high-hydration, types of bread (e.g. GF breads).

oven setup
preheat the oven for at least half an hour. use the oven fan function; it distributes heat better, and helps with evaporation.
use an oven thermometer, if you have one, for better accuracy.

pan prep
line the pan with baking paper, or grease it (messier).
here's how i make my custom-fit lining templates, which make the job so much easier.

placement
make sure to position the loaf in the center of the oven.
if your oven has uneven heat distribution, rotate the pan during bake.

humidity
before turning on the oven, place a second pan at the bottom of the oven, filled with some water.
alternatively, during bake, use a spray bottle to spray water into the oven space and onto the side walls.
make sure to turn the oven fan off before opening the door!
