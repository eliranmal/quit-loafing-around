mixing methods are different kinds of procedures used by bakers in the mixing step of the bread making process.

here are some of the ones used here:


straight dough
  1. mix the liquids, sugars, yeast and flours (in that order)
  2. knead for about 5 minutes
  3. add fats and salt
  4. knead for another 3 minutes
  5. add any chuncked extras (nuts, raisins, cubed veggies etc.)
  6. knead until they're evenly incorporated

sponge n’ dough
  1. mix some of the flour, water and yeast. some recipes also include sugars, salts, or milk (solids)
  2. cover and set to rest until the dough starts to deflate (decrements in height)
  3. add the rest of the ingredients, mix and knead

here's a generic sponge formula:

  • 65 flours
  • 2 active dry yeast
  • 50 water (or milk)
all quantities should be reduced from the main formula

folding
  1. mix all ingredients
  2. cover and set to rest for 10 minutes
  3. fold the dough by lifting a corner and pulling it towards the center, repeat 15-20 times
  4. repeat steps 2-3 two more times
