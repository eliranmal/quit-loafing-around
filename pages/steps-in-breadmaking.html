--- layout: page title: "the 12 steps of bread" permalink: /steps-in-breadmaking/ ---

there are some common steps involved in the making of any bread, and each is as important as the next.
here’s the general algorithm:


1. scaling
mise en place.
measure everything by weight (not volume), for accuracy.

2. mixing, autolyse, kneading
mix the ingredients, put to rest to hydrate the flours (optional), then knead it.
note: fats go in last.

3. bulk fermentation
leave covered for initial proofing.
until the dough nearly doubles in size.

4. folding (“punching”)
fold the dough several times, then back to proofing. can be repeated couple of more times.
this helps in strengthening the gluten net. also, no need to really punch your dough, ok?

5. dividing
if you’re making more than one loaf, or lots of buns / rolls / bagels.
use a scale to weigh every piece.

6. pre-shaping
fold the dough to shape.
the technique can change according to the desired loaf type.

7. benching
leave the shaped dough to rest on the bench.
to proof some more, and to relax the gluten net.

8. final shaping (“panning”)
fold the dough to shape again, and put it in the pan (or on the peel).

9. final proofing
leave to proof in the pan.
until the spring test passes.

10. scoring
coat with flour, or glaze with some liquid and cover with seeds, and score the bread as necessary.
score depth depends on dough hydration, but the shape is up to you :)

11. baking
place it in the center of a pre-heated fan oven.
generally, yeast breads will bake at mid-high temperatures, and sourdough breads at high. some breads will benefit from a short initial high-temperature phase (e.g. GF ones).

12. cooling
take the bread out of the pan, and leave it to cool on a cooling rack.
this is actually post-baking and de-hydration, and can take anywhere between an hour to an entire day.
